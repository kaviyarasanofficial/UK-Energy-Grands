A Bury energy consultant is on a mission to help residents reduce the price of their bills during the cost of living crisis.

Zishan Ellahi, a consultant with Bury-based company UK Energy Grants, says help and support for those looking to reduce their bills is out there – but many people still don’t know they’re eligible. The consultant says his team’s biggest challenge is convincing people that financial support for energy saving solutions is legitimate and available to many who could benefit from them. UK Energy Grants, who were recently awarded the North West Energy Consultancy of the Year gong at the Energy Efficiency awards, began trading in 2021 with the aim of helping people access the support to which they are entitled. It provides advice about a range of available grants and services including insulation, heating upgrades and renewable energy. READ MORE: Households bills and insurance costs see big jump for 2023 Zishan said: “Every week we speak to families who have no electricity and no gas in Bury. “Getting people aware and convincing them that these grants are real, that’s the biggest job right now. “The government is investing so much more into making homes more energy efficient. “The common question is ‘have you got help to pay my bills?’ we try and show people how to make their properties more efficient.” As well as advice about grants the company also offer surveys to help people find out how energy efficient their homes and Zishan says demand his company’s help had soared. “It’s 10 times busier than this time last year simply because of the rise in energy costs,” he added. READ MORE: Passover meals supplied by Prestwich charity in cost of living crisis His advice to anyone looking for ways to reduce their bills would be to do their research on the deals that are out there. He said: "If you see an ad, check it out." The company's success in the North West means they have now opened an office in Plymouth and Zishan says he couldn't have done it without the hard work of his colleagues, Jacky and Daryl. Single mum's damp and cold home in Saltash transformed - for free

A mum from the Saltash area says her family's life has changed for the better after coming across UK Energy Grants, a company which helped her at no cost to herself. Tammy Jemmett had been trying to find help for two years, so she could secure a grant to heat her property. The 40-year-old said she was forced to fill her two children's baths with kettle water as she had no running hot water and relied on her electric heaters and power shower, but was worried about the cost. Tammy also said her house was filled with damp due to the lack of heating in their home. Tammy said she came across a social media post advertising UK Energy Grants and she was "reluctant" at first, as she was worried there would be some "small print" that she hadn't be made aware of. But Tammy says she was reassured by the company and given all the details needed to make her feel comfortable to take the next step. She said: "For two years I’d been trying to find a company who would help me get the grant to allow me to have a new central heating system, my very old back boiler wasn’t working. I had no heating or hot water. "All we had was electric heaters and the electric power shower. I had no loft insulation so my home could be cold and damp. I was reluctant [to speak to UK Energy Grants]. "One, because I’d applied and spoken to other companies who said they could help, but they never got back in contact. Secondly, I was worried that at the end of all the work being done, there would have been some small print that I had missed or had not been told about." Tammy added that she was worried she "would have to pay for the work". "Being a single mum who suffers from chronic illness I don’t have spare money to have paid for it," she added. However, Tammy says there were no hidden surprises and she now has a brand new combi boiler and four new radiators in her home, as well as new insulation put into her loft. "It hasn't cost me anything," she said. "Myself and my children are now warm and not living in a cold damp house anymore. I can already feel all this work has helped with my illness too." Tammy is now urging people to get in touch with the UK Energy Grants team if they need help. She said: "The work for me, all I had to do was prove what my household income and what benefits I was on and they did the rest. "They kept me informed of how the process was going and when I should get my work done. The work was done in a quick and efficient time too." UK Energy Grants website states they are a "team of experts who work in the efficiency industry helping people increase the energy efficiency of their homes". The website adds: "We are a team of consultants who work directly with funders in the Eco Scheme. We have recently been recognized as a Highly Commended Energy Consultancy in our industry. "We have partnered with over 50 installers across the UK. Everyone we work with is registered with the government approval body known as Trustmark."
